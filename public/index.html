Ruth S. Collins

Realist Landscape and Portrait Artist

Ruth has always been an artist, since she displayed talent as a child. She loved the diverse landscape of the relatively small peninsula bordered by the Chesapeake Bay, Potomac and Patuxent Rivers. Farmers, watermen, Amish, historic buildings, and military operations all provide her with inspiration. She paints in acrylics and pastels. In recent years she has switched from oils because of the time it takes for them to dry and to be varnished. Her subject matter is a matter of principle to her. She wants to show scenes of real life that might tell a story or in some way be compelling to the viewer. Almost all of her work is from her photographs, taken usually from the car, during a walk, or some other place where the candid camera is employed.
